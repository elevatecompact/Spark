# Compliance Framework

Spark maintains compliance with major industry standards and regulations including SOC2, GDPR, CCPA, ISO 27001, and SOC3. This document outlines the controls and practices that enable compliance.

## SOC 2 (Type II)

Spark undergoes annual SOC 2 Type II audits for the Security, Availability, and Confidentiality trust criteria. Key controls include:

- Access control and identity management
- Change management and release processes
- Logical and physical security controls
- System monitoring and incident response
- Risk management and vendor due diligence
- Encryption standards and key management

## GDPR Compliance

Spark is fully compliant with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for European users:

- **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** — Available to all customers
- **Data Subject Rights** — Automated tools for access, rectification, erasure, portability, and objection
- **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** — Appointed and registered
- **Breach Notification** — 72-hour notification commitment for reportable breaches
- **Data Retention** — Configurable retention policies with automated deletion
- **Data Residency** — EU data stored in EU-based data centers

## ISO 27001

Spark is ISO 27001:2022 certified. The Information Security Management System (ISMS) covers all systems, processes, and personnel. Internal audits are conducted quarterly, and external audits occur annually.

## Other Frameworks

- **CCPA** — California Consumer Privacy Act compliance tools for US users
- **PCI DSS** — Level 1 compliance (validated annually) for payment processing
- **HIPAA** — Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) available; BA-configured instance supports PHI

## Compliance Automation

Compliance evidence is collected automatically through infrastructure-as-code scanning, configuration audits, and continuous control monitoring. This reduces audit preparation time and provides real-time compliance posture visibility.
